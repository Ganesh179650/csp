Healthy living tips

Small changes that boost everyday wellness

Keep these evidence-based tips on your fridge or share them on your community WhatsApp groups so everyone benefits from a consistent routine.

Balance your plate

Fill half the plate with fruits or vegetables, a quarter with whole grains, and a quarter with protein. Add a spoon of healthy fats like groundnut or sesame oil.

Move every hour

Use phone reminders to stretch, walk, or climb stairs for 3 minutes every hour. This improves circulation and reduces screen fatigue.

Sleep hygiene

Dim lights an hour before bedtime, avoid caffeine post 4 PM, and keep phones away from the pillow to improve deep sleep cycles.

Daily routine ideas

  1. 6:00 AM – Hydration & breathwork: Start with warm water + lemon and 5 deep breathing rounds to wake up the lungs.
  2. 7:00 AM – Movement: 20 minutes brisk walk or skipping plus 5 minutes of stretching focusing on neck, shoulders, and spine.
  3. 12:30 PM – Mindful meal: Eat away from screens, chew slowly, and include a probiotic (curd or fermented drink).
  4. 4:00 PM – Refuel & connect: Grab a fruit or nuts, check posture, and send a wellness check-in message to a neighbor.
  5. 9:30 PM – Wind down: Journal three gratitudes, plan tomorrow’s meals, and keep water ready for the morning.

Health & hygiene awareness

Simple habits that keep the neighborhood safer

Share these reminders with family and neighbors so everyone knows how to prepare for seasonal illnesses, reduce infections, and keep common areas clean.

Hand hygiene

20-second wash rule

Scrub palms, between fingers, under nails, and wrists for at least 20 seconds. Use foot-operated taps at clinics when available and carry a small sanitizer when traveling.

Safe hydration

Drink & store water safely

Boil or filter water during monsoon alerts, use covered containers, and clean bottles daily to avoid bacterial buildup.

Community spaces

Disinfect shared surfaces

Wipe door handles, benches, and play equipment twice a day during flu season. Ventilate indoor halls every hour to improve air quality.

Daily hygiene checklist

  • Carry your own water bottle and reusable mask to all programs.
  • Cover coughs/sneezes with the inside of your elbow or a tissue.
  • Separate waste into dry, wet, and biomedical bins at community venues.
  • Keep a small first-aid pouch with ORS, bandages, and prescribed meds.
